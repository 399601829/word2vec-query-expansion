*Granby* is a city in southwestern Quebec </wiki/Quebec>, located east
of Montreal </wiki/Montreal>. The population as of the Canada 2011
Census </wiki/Canada_2011_Census> was 63,433. Granby is the seat of La
Haute-Yamaska Regional County Municipality
</wiki/La_Haute-Yamaska_Regional_County_Municipality>. It is the fourth
most populated city in Montérégie </wiki/Mont%C3%A9r%C3%A9gie> after
Longueuil </wiki/Longueuil>, Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu
</wiki/Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu>, and Brossard </wiki/Brossard>. The
city is named after John Manners, Marquess of Granby
</wiki/John_Manners,_Marquess_of_Granby>.

    History

</wiki/File:View_of_the_town_of_Granby_-_1883.jpg>
</wiki/File:View_of_the_town_of_Granby_-_1883.jpg>
The town of Granby seen in 1883.

Granby area was inhabited by the First Nations </wiki/First_Nations>
until the late 18th century.^[7] <#cite_note-Laurion-7> On October 5,
1760, Major Robert Rogers </wiki/Robert_Rogers_(soldier)>, with a
company of 200 men launched punitive expedition burning everything in
its path.^[7] <#cite_note-Laurion-7>

In 1792, Loyalists </wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)> were granted
permission to colonize the Eastern Townships </wiki/Eastern_Townships>.
In January 29, 1803, the Executive Council of Quebec
</wiki/Executive_Council_of_Quebec> conceded the /Township of Granby/ to
Colonel Henry Caldwell </wiki/Henry_Caldwell> and his 97 associates.^[7]
<#cite_note-Laurion-7> John Horner, the first inhabitant who settled on
the site of the current city arrived in 1813. Horner built a sawmill
near the Yamaska River </wiki/Yamaska_River>. Twelve years later, in
1825, he opened a general store with Richard Frost.^[7]
<#cite_note-Laurion-7> Frost traced the official plans later that same
year.^[8] <#cite_note-Gendron-8>

Granby became an incorporated municipality in 1816 and a city in 1971.

In May 2006, the citizens of Granby and Granby Township
</wiki/Granby_(township),_Quebec> held a referendum on a possible
merger. A majority of citizens of the two municipalities approved the
merger, which took effect on January 1, 2007.^[9] <#cite_note-9>


    Geography

Granby is located approximately halfway between Montreal
</wiki/Montreal> and Sherbrooke </wiki/Sherbrooke> along Autoroute 10
</wiki/Quebec_Autoroute_10>. Granby is crossed by the Yamaska River
</wiki/Yamaska_River> that is a tributary </wiki/Tributary> of Lake
Boivin </w/index.php?title=Lake_Boivin&action=edit&redlink=1>.

    Economy & Attractions

Granby is a regional center for industries (textile, lumber, dairy
products) and commercial zone, but is also a touristic city, due to the
presence of the important Granby Zoo </wiki/Granby_Zoo>, founded by the
Mayor Horace Boivin. It hosts a /Fête des Mascottes/ ("Mascot Festival")
each year in July.

Granby is the host of the yearly "Festival de la chanson de Granby"
("Granby Song Festival") in which many well-known artists, such as Jean
Leloup </wiki/Jean_Leloup> and Luc De Larochellière
</w/index.php?title=Luc_De_Larochelli%C3%A8re&action=edit&redlink=1>,
have first gained public exposure.

Granby also hosts the international classic car show with over 2500
antiques, vintage, hot rods, muscle cars and classic imports cars every
summer. Granby's annual classic car show
<http://www.cyberpresse.ca/images/bizphotos/435x290/201007/30/189130-international-voitures-anciennes-granby-attire.jpg>

Granby is also home to the Galeries de Granby </wiki/Galeries_de_Granby>
regional shopping mall </wiki/Shopping_mall> which has 106 stores.^[12]
<#cite_note-Westcliff-12>


    Sports

Granby is the location of the annual Challenger Banque Nationale de
Granby </wiki/Challenger_Banque_Nationale_de_Granby> of the ATP
Challenger Tour </wiki/ATP_Challenger_Tour>, which takes place at the
Club de Tennis des Loisirs de Granby
</wiki/Club_de_Tennis_des_Loisirs_de_Granby>.

The Granby Inouk </wiki/Granby_Inouk> of the Quebec Junior AAA Hockey
League </wiki/Quebec_Junior_AAA_Hockey_League> are based in Granby. The
Inouk play their home games at the Leonard Grondin Arena
</wiki/Ar%C3%A9na_Leonard_Grondin>.

Granby is also home to the Ligue de Baseball Senior Élite du Québec
</wiki/Ligue_de_Baseball_Senior_%C3%89lite_du_Qu%C3%A9bec>'s Granby
Guerriers </w/index.php?title=Granby_Guerriers&action=edit&redlink=1>.
The Guerriers play their home games at Napoléon Fontaine Stadium
</w/index.php?title=Napol%C3%A9on_Fontaine_Stadium&action=edit&redlink=1>.

From 1981 to 1997, Granby was home to a QMJHL </wiki/QMJHL> team,
originally called the Granby Bisons </wiki/Granby_Bisons> but changed to
the Granby Prédateurs </wiki/Granby_Pr%C3%A9dateurs> in 1995. The team
won the Memorial Cup </wiki/Memorial_Cup> in 1996. In 1997, the
franchise relocated to Sydney, Nova Scotia </wiki/Sydney,_Nova_Scotia>
and became the Cape Breton Screaming Eagles
</wiki/Cape_Breton_Screaming_Eagles>. Both teams played at Leonard
Grondin Arena </wiki/Ar%C3%A9na_Leonard_Grondin>.


    Transportation

The public transit department of the city is Transport urbain Granby
</wiki/Transport_urbain_Granby>. Until 1956, it was the eastern terminus
of the Montreal and Southern Counties Railway
</wiki/Montreal_and_Southern_Counties_Railway>'s Montreal-Granby Line.
